    • Le Labyrinthe de L'obscurite

    • High Concept: You must navigate through the dark maze with limited visibility in order to find the hidden treasure.
    • Theme: Person who glows in the dark.
    • Mandated Variety: Input: Keyboard Randomness: No randomness Genre: Overhead, puzzle, side scroller (maybe?) Play Style: Explorer, achiever
    • Player Experience Goals: Players will have a fun challenge trying to navigate through the maze with an exciting twist of only being able to see a short distance around themselves due to their ability to glow in the dark!
    • Gameplay: TThe player will use the arrow keys to navigate around the maze.
    • Prototype Goal: Tilemaps, graphics and masking, bitmapdata from tileset data (not going well xD)
    • Features: Currently there are a limited amount of features with this game. I spent the majority of my time trying to adjust the game world scale in order to get the effect I wanted with limited visibility, but as Professor Gingold and I both noticed, it is apparently a known and unsolved issue with scaling the game world causing collision issues all across the board. There are a couple of workarounds I am planning on trying for my iteration, but will require me to recreate the map of my game entirely which is why I didn't have the time to implement that in this game, so instead I simply made the window size smaller.
    • Setting/Premise: The game is certainly more abstract, and doesn't necessarily have a specific setting. You are an great explorer due to your ability to glow in the dark, and you have finally discovered a famous maze that according to legend holds a very large treasure.
    • Story: You have discovered a famous maze that according to legend holds a very large treasure. Being the explorer you are, you decide to wander in hoping to best the maze and claim the treasure for your own.
    • Target Audience: Young children
    • Play Time: 1-3 Minutes (Hard to guess since I made the maze and therefor know it like the back of my hand)
    • Strategies: Players will have to try and create the map of the maze in their head to successfully navigate it without backtracking over and over.
